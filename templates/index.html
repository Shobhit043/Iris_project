Virginica

Iris virginica, native to North America, is recognized for its large and showy blue to violet flowers, often with yellow or white highlights. Thriving in wetland areas, this species, commonly known as the Virginia iris, displays sword-shaped leaves and contributes to the biodiversity of aquatic ecosystems. It is both admired for its ornamental beauty and valued for its ecological role.

Versicolor

Iris versicolor, commonly known as the blue flag iris, is a North American species with striking blue to violet flowers and distinctive yellow or white markings. Thriving in wetland habitats, this iris features sword-like leaves and is valued for its vibrant appearance. It serves as both an ornamental plant and an essential part of natural wetland ecosystems.

Setosa

Iris setosa is a species of iris plant characterized by its distinctive blue-violet flowers with striking yellow markings. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in various climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental beauty. The plant's unique appearance makes it easily recognizable among iris species.

About Dataset

The Iris dataset was used in R.A. Fisher's classic 1936 paper, The Use of Multiple Measurements in Taxonomic Problems, and can also be found on the UCI Machine Learning Repository. It includes three iris species with 50 samples each as well as some properties about each flower. One flower species is linearly separable from the other two, but the other two are not linearly separable from each other.

The columns in this dataset are:

  • Id
  • SepalLengthCm (4 - 8)
  • SepalWidthCm (2 - 5)
  • PetalLengthCm (1 - 7)
  • PetalWidthCm (0.1 - 3)
  • Species
